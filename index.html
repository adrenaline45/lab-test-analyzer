Who are we? Meet the team behind LTA

Typically, “normal” or reference ranges are generated using a method called normoreferencing. That is, data from all the people who have taken the test are combined in a statistical analysis, and the middle 95% of the data points are considered “normal” or “in reference range.”

Because different lab companies use different machines and may test people in different geographical regions, each company develops their own reference range.

Having a normal or in reference range lab results may not mean you are healthy, especially because most people only get blood tests when they are sick.

For these reasons, our science team provides “optimal” ranges, based on current scientific knowledge about each marker and its significance for ensuring your long-term health.

The final result is a product that can not only tell you if your levels are “normal,” but which can also help you further tweak and refine your lifestyle and diet to minimize your risk of disease and maximize your well-being.
